Oscar was 15 years old. He lived in a small apartment in the city that felt too quiet, like a house holding its breath. His life used to be normal and warm, like a sunny afternoon that never seemed like it would end. But then his parents were found guilty in court and sent to prison. After that, everything changed.

At school, whispers followed him down the hallways like shadows that wouldn’t leave him alone. The looks from other students felt like small stones being thrown at his back. Oscar felt ashamed and angry. The shame sat on his shoulders like a heavy backpack he couldn’t take off. Even though he had done nothing wrong, guilt grew inside him like a weed in a garden.
